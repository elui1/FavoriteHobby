So what is this game?


Go is a board game that originated in China thousands of years ago. Two players alternate placing black and white stones on the intersections of a board consisting of a grid of 19x19 lines. The object of the game is to surround more territory than your opponent. Sounds easy enough, right?


Brief History


Since its conception, Go was considered one of the four arts of the Chinese scholar. Emperors employed the game as a means of settling quarrels and disputes, as well as a blueprint for military strategy. During more modern times, Go spread throughout Asia like wildfire, first to Japan, then gradually to South Korea and Taiwan. Go first appeared in the West more than a hundred years ago and is steadily growing in popularity. Today, there are many thousands of players in North America and an even greater number in Europe. The estimated number of players worldwide is 50 million.
