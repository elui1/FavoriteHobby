"When I was young, . . . I wanted to be the world's best Go player." -- Bill Gates


"While the Baroque rules of chess could only have been created by humans, the rules of go are so elegant, organic, and rigorously logical that if intelligent life forms exist elsewhere in the universe, they almost certainly play go." -- Edward Lasker, chess grandmaster


"{it is} something unearthly . . . If there are sentient beings on other planets, then they play Go." - Emanuel Lasker, chess world champion

  1. Go is easy to learn.

    The simple rules of the game allow you to pick up the game at any age. In fact, young children can become very good very quickly.


  2. Go is the hardest game there is.

    Enthusiasts often state that there are more possible games of Go than atoms in the universe.


  3. If you get good, others recognize it.

    As a professional, other players and those who have heard of the game respect me and think I am really smart.


  4. If you get good, you get to do cool things.

    In my Go adventures, I've snorkeled in the warm oceans of Maui, climbed the Great Wall of China, spent time in some of the nicest hotels on the planet, and sang karaoke with legends of the game.


  5. You get to meet great people.

    The universality of the game transcends geographic, cultural and language barriers. Some of my favorite people in the world are thousands of miles away.
